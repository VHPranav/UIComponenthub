## Overview

The **Kinetic Balance** background is a study in physical simulation and material rendering. It features a diagonal staircase of heavy glass planks that respond to a continuous procedural wave. The component uses custom **Spring Physics** to ensure that when one plank moves, the entire system adjusts to maintain an "equilibrium" state, creating a satisfyngly heavy and interconnected feel.

## Features

- **MeshPhysicalMaterial**: High-fidelity glass rendering with transmission, thickness, and clearcoat properties.
- **Spring Dynamics**: Custom spring-damper physics for smooth, elastic movements.
- **Mass-Linkage Logic**: The system calculates a global "compensator" value to keep the center of mass stable during the wave animation.
- **Procedural Ripples**: A sine-wave based trigger that travels through the indices of the planks, simulating a continuous step-through.
- **Performance Optimized**: Uses instance-safe material disposal and efficient geometry management.

## How it works

1. **Materials**: Each plank uses `THREE.MeshPhysicalMaterial` with `transmission: 0.9` and `thickness: 0.5`. This allows light to pass through the objects, creating realistic glass refraction.
2. **The Wave**: A procedural wave is calculated every frame using `Math.sin(time - index * offset)`. When the wave value exceeds a threshold, the specific plank's `targetY` is lowered.
3. **Equilibrium Calculation**: To make the planks feel "linked," the component sums the deviation of all planks from their base positions. It then applies a negative "compensator" force to all planks, ensuring the average position remains constant.
4. **Spring Physics**: The movement isn't linear; it uses a proportional force (`target - current`) multiplied by a spring constant and softened by a damping factor.
